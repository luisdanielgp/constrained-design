As a part of the Devf Batch 17 program, I gathered a team of students from different courses (back-end, front-end and intermediate levels) to participate in the course final hackathon. The task was to create an MVP of an app idea through a design sprint in one week. My participation as the team leader lead me to get involved in the product design, and more specifically in the back-end development of the app since that was the course I was taking at the time.

The process

After several weeks of trainning, the hackathon was announced so I pitched an idea that I was previously working with some college friends. My idea attracted Devf's students from the back-end and front-end courses, so after explaining the general concept we started working on it.

The first days of the week we applied the design sprint methodology, our ideas evolved and we ended up with a lo-fi prototype of the MVP.

Once the portotype and wireframe were ready I focused on the back-end of the web app. I designed and implemented several functionalities in order to create an MVP that could show the judges how the platform worked. After programming the back-end I helped the integration of the front-end with Django templates.

As an extra, we deployed the project on Heroku to make it easier to show at the final presentation. The prototype can still be found on this url.

The outcome of that first software development project of us was the one selected as the winner of the hackathon by the Devf staff.

The app

The main concept of RewardMe was to create an app to reward users that donated to nonprofit campaigns. It displayed many NGO's profiles showing their work and needs, and allowing users to donate to their causes. The donors received discount coupons previously authorized by popular local businesses such as restaurants and coffee shops.

The views developed for the app include the user login, user's list of donations and list of coupons, the NGO list, NGO detail and the landing page.

The stack used for this project was the following:

  • Django 2.0
  • Postgres
  • CSS 3
  • HTML 5
  • Bootstrap 4
