Shoe organizer

Designers

LD Guzmán / Valery Martínez / Pedro González

Year

2019

A project developed after researching the customers of Truper's physical stores located in many mexican cities. Truper is a 60 year old mexican company that manufactures and distributes power tools and builder's hardware. The project was focused on the creation of new ways for empowering the customer to build DIY utilitarian objects for their daily usage.

The process

After field research and alternatives exploration, we came up with the idea of creating pop-up stands with the new DIY strategy branding and printed manuals that instructed the customer to build different kinds of objects. This stands would be positioned in strategic parts of the stores depending on the tools needed for the built. The prototyped objects where a shoe organizer, a couple of shelves and a stool. All of them fabricated with affordable plywood and simple finishes.

The assembly manual

All the cuts and assemblies needed where designed to be performed by consumer grade tools in order to make it accesible for a wide variety of customers and allow the brand to promote their products.
